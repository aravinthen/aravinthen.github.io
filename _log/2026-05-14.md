## Summary

## Notes
### Literature
1. Ah... so the comparison is basically just marginalized over fixed opponent actions and compared to an average of all moves that could be taken. That's a pretty strong baselining approach, almost like a mean-field approach in a sense, although the paper discusses it with respect to a "default" action of sorts.
2. This counterfactual baseline is the main innovation here. It's *really* damned smart...
3. Oooh, I see the benefit with the counterfactual advantage discussed here. You can basically update the baseline for all agents in a single pass, which makes it quite an efficient algorithm...
4. What I'm *less* convinced by is how efficient it is to train this centralized critic with a baseline inclusion. It's quite a complicated architecture as it is without considering scaling applications!
5. I suppose that the essence of this algorithm lies in enhancing cooperation. I'm still a little confused as to how the marginalization *encourages* cooperation... I'll have to think about this a little more.
