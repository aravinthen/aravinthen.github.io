### Literature 
1. "plus an error term, which makes Q produce z’s that can reproduce a given X; this term will become small if Q is high-capacity" - what does high capacity mean?
2. The **evidence lower-bound* (ELBO) has a very interesting form. There are two basic components to it:
    * Reconstruction loss, which is how well the decoder reconstructs the original input data from the latent variable,
    * Regularization loss, which is the KL divergence between the approximated latent distribution (this is what I'm assuming $$Q$$ in this paper is) and the prior distribution.
