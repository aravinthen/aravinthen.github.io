## Summary
1. Spent some time developing an overview of foundation models and the technical side of actually engineering them for reasoning.

## Foundation models
1. This is going to be a journey.
2. As far as I see it, there can be no real work on foundation models without actively trying to understand the transformer. Transformers are the absolute basis of this field - the raw material that is used to create all of the amazing work we've seen in the last four years. 
3. I'm going to break my process into four phases: 
    * Becoming extremely comfortable with transformers, including their development and training, 
    * Learn post-training and the techniques used to improve a basic transformer into a powerful model,
    * Moving into reasoning research - the real frontier.
4. I've already tried to build a transformer and struggled quite a bit. It didn't really make any theoretical sense to me at the time and simply constructing a transformer was messy and required huge leaps of thought. My goal over the next week will be to nail down the foundations of what makes a transformer work. By the end of my exercise, I want to be able to treat transformers as basic objects.
