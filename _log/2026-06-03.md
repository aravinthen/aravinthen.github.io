## Summary
* Started reading *Deep Counterfactual Regret Minimization* by Brown et al. 

## Notes
### Literature
1. I guess it's pretty evident from the title that the goal of this paper is to replace the manual tabular CFR with a function approximation variant.
2. *"However, most popular RL algorithms do not converge to good policies (equilibria) in imperfect-information games in theory or in practice."* - damning!
3. I mean... all that's going on here is that a neural network is used to predict the regrets, as well as another neural network designed to approximate the strategy that the regret predictor converges to.
4. So the authors introduce a massive memory buffer to store advantage data... the interesting thing here is that the neural network is retrained from scratch with data that steadily accumulates. That's... interesting. Is this sort of a highly manual offline reinforcement learning approach?
5. *Oooooh. Very interesting:* the exploitability of the agent is bounded by the accuracy of the underlying network. That's unexpected!
